I am a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Oslo, Norway, working with the Digital Signal Processing and Image Analysis (DSB) research group. My main research focus is on the Full-Waveform Inversion (FWI) method, with applications ranging from non-destructive seismic imaging to medical ultrasound elastography. My work lies at the intersection of applied mathematics, geophysics, signal processing, and medical imaging. Previously, I was a postdoctoral researcher with the IDEFIX team in France, a joint research group between Inria Saclay Centre and EDF R&D. I obtained my PhD in Applied Mathematics from the University of Caen Normandy, France.

FULLELASTO ‚Äî Full-Waveform Inversion for Time-Harmonic Elastography Using Ultrasound Shear Waves (2024‚Äì2027)

Funding: MSCA COFUND (European Union & University of Oslo) ‚Äî Postdoctoral Fellow Profile

This research is part of the Ultrasound Shear Wave Elastography Project within the DSB group . The project aims to advance medical ultrasound elastography for estimating tissue mechanical properties, which are essential for tumor detection and disease staging. The elastography team within DSB group focuses on developing ultrasound shear-wave elastography methods using external time-harmonic excitations, with the long-term goal of reconstructing three-dimensional myocardial elasticity maps of the heart. My specific research project focuses on integrating Full-Waveform Inversion (FWI) into shear-wave elastography by explicitly incorporating the physics of wave propagation into the inversion process.

Seismic Imaging of the Dam‚ÄìRock Foundation Interface

Funding: Plan France Relance (2022‚Äì2024). This project was initiated during my previous postdoctoral position with the IDEFIX team in France.

It is a collaborative project between INRIA and EDF R&D, addressing interface imaging problems at electricity production sites. The primary case study focuses on imaging the dam‚Äìrock foundation interface using non-destructive seismic waves, a critical component for ensuring structural stability. Our approach combines shape optimization techniques with the Full-Waveform Inversion framework to obtain high-resolution images of the interface from seismic measurements acquired non-invasively on the dam wall.
