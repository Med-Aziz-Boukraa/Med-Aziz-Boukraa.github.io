I am a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Oslo, Norway, working with the Digital Signal Processing and Image Analysis (DSB) research group. My main research focus is on the Full-Waveform Inversion (FWI) method, with applications ranging from non-destructive seismic imaging to medical ultrasound elastography. Full-Waveform Inversion for Time-Harmonic Elastography using Ultrasound Shear Waves

University of Oslo â€” Ultrasound Elastography Project
DScience/DSTrain: Postdoctoral Fellow Profile

This research is part of the Ultrasound Shear Wave Elastography Project at DSB group, which aims to advance medical ultrasound elastography for estimating tissue mechanical propertiesâ€”essential for tumor detection and disease staging. Our group is particularly interested in developing methods for ultrasound shear-wave elastography using external time-harmonic excitations, with the goal of creating 3D myocardial elasticity maps of the heart.

My specific project focuses on integrating Full-Waveform Inversion into the processing of shear-waves by incorporating the physics of wave propagation.

Seismic imaging of dam-rock foundation interface

This project started during my previous postdoctoral position at IDEFIX team. It consists in a collaborative work between INRIA and EDF R&D. The project addresses interface imaging problems in electricity production sites. The primary case study focuses on imaging the damâ€“rock foundation interface, using non-destructive seismic waves. This interface is a critical factor for ensuring structural stability.

Our approach combines shape optimization techniques with the Full-Waveform Inversion framework to obtain high-resolution images of the interface from seismic measurements acquired non-invasively on the dam wall. Currently, we are working on expanding this methodologies in 3D and improving the forward model to make it applicable in operational conditions.
