ℹ️ About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Oslo, Norway, working with the Digital Signal Processing and Image Analysis (DSB) research group. I develop physics-based inverse methods for imaging applications across geophysics and medical ultrasound.

My work sits at the intersection of applied mathematics, geophysics, signal processing, and medical imaging. Previously, I was a postdoctoral researcher with the IDEFIX team in France, a joint research group between Inria Saclay Centre and EDF R&D. I obtained my PhD in Applied Mathematics from the University of Caen Normandy, France.

🧑‍🔬 Current Projects

Ongoing work spans medical elastography and seismic imaging, with a shared focus on practical, physics-based inversion methods.

FULLELASTO: Full-Waveform Inversion for Time-Harmonic Elastography Using Ultrasound Shear Waves

2024-2027

Funding: MSCA COFUND (European Union & University of Oslo)

Hosted by the DSB group, FULLELASTO is part of the Ultrasound Shear Wave Elastography project. The project aims to develop robust, physics-based inversion workflows to improve tissue characterization and disease staging in clinical ultrasound.

  • Integrate Full-Waveform Inversion into ultrasound shear-wave elastography workflows.
  • Improve reconstruction robustness through explicit wave-physics constraints.
  • Enable higher-fidelity 3D myocardial elasticity mapping.

Seismic Imaging of the Dam-Rock Foundation Interface

2022-2027

Funding: Plan France Relance (2022-2024), followed by AURORA mobility funding (2026-2027)

Initiated with the IDEFIX team as a collaboration between INRIA and EDF R&D, this project addresses interface imaging challenges at electricity production sites.

  • Targets high-resolution imaging of the dam-rock foundation interface.
  • Combines shape optimization with Full-Waveform Inversion for non-invasive seismic data.
  • Extends methodology to 3D settings and more realistic operational forward models.

AURORA Programme

The AURORA programme is a Norway-France mobility scheme that supports researcher exchanges, joint workshops, and collaboration building between partner institutions. The supported project is titled Full-Waveform Inversion for Dam Safety.
